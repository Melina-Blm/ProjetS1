GLOSSARY

Feminism :

A set of philosophical movements and ideas whose purpose is to define and promote equality between women and men by advocating for women's rights in civil society and in private life.

Sexism :

Discriminatory attitudes based on a person's sex and/or gender.

Resistance :

The act of physically resisting someone or a group.

Stereotypes :

Expression or ready-made opinion, without any originality and often pejorative.

Patriarchy :

A form of social organization in which men exercise power in all areas.

NCC :

National Cinema Center (aims to encourage the promotion and distribution of works to a wide audience)..

Equality in law :

Equality before the law or equality in law is the principle that every human being should be treated equally by the law. No individual or group of individuals should therefore have privileges guaranteed by law.

Gender equality :

Each sex is represented equally in institutions. It is an instrument for equality, which consists in ensuring that women and men have access to the same opportunities and rights.

TIMELINE

1896

Alice Guy Blaché becomes the first woman director in the world.

1910

Alice Guy created the Solax Film Company, one of the most important production companies of the 1910s.

1911

Lois Weber (1879 - 1939) became the most daring filmmaker of her time. She put forward taboo subjects such as the death penalty, equal pay and abortion.

1926

Lotte Reiniger worked on one of the very first animated films.

1930

Screenwriter Frances Marion became the first woman to win an Oscar for Best Adaptation and Best Original Story.

1970

The Women's Steering Committee was formed by six women directors who were concerned about equality.

1972

In 1972, the Edinburgh International Film Festival proposed the Women's Event: it was the first section devoted solely to women filmmakers.

1975

Laura Mulvey is considered the founder of feminist theory on cinema. She denounces the way in which cinema contributes to male domination.

The female characters, existing only to provide visual pleasure to the male audience, are passive objects looked at while their male partners are active looking subjects.

1976

The actress Delphine Seyrig joins the feminist fights and questions the representation of women in cinema (often perceived as a desire).

2010

Kathryn Bigelow becomes the first and only woman to win a Best Director Oscar for her film Demineurs.

2017

Women are increasingly represented in action films especially with the release of Wonder Woman.
